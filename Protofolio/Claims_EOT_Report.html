5 Time Impact Analysis Methodology

All delay claims were analyzed using the Time Impact Analysis (TIA) method per AACE 29R-03 and SCL Delay & Disruption Protocol.

Analysis Protocol

StepDescriptionTool/Method
1. Baseline ValidationConfirm approved baseline schedule (fragnet) for impacted areaPrimavera P6 — Baseline Log
2. As-Built WindowExtract as-built data for the delay window from daily reportsSite diaries, DPRs
3. Impact FragnetCreate detailed fragnet of delay-causing event and its logic tiesPrimavera P6 — Fragnet
4. Insertion into ScheduleInsert fragnet into contemporaneous schedule updateP6 schedule model
5. Impact MeasurementMeasure delta between scheduled completion before/after insertionCritical path analysis
6. Concurrency CheckIdentify any concurrent contractor delays during the same windowAs-built vs. as-planned overlay
7. Entitlement CalculationDetermine net EOT entitlement after concurrency deductionSCL Protocol rules
8. Cost AssessmentQuantity prolongation and disruption cost entitlementContract rates + Hudson formula

7 Dispute Avoidance & Mitigation

Recommendations to minimize future claims and avoid escalation to formal dispute resolution.

✓ Early Warning System
Implement a 30-day early warning register for potential delay events. All RFIs, design clarifications, and approval requests to be logged with response time tracking. Monthly review with Q Design and Takamul. Target: reduce employer response time from 18 days to 10 days.
✓ Change Order Fast-Track
For future design changes, implement a 14-day fast-track review cycle. COs to be assessed for schedule AND cost impact simultaneously (not sequentially). Proposed change to Contract Clause 13.3 procedures.
✓ Monthly Progress Meeting — Claims Review
Dedicated claims review agenda item during monthly progress meetings. All pending EOT applications to be tracked with action owners and resolution deadlines. Current pending claims: CL-003 (14d), CL-004 (28d).
✓ Schedule Recovery Incentive
Propose a recovery incentive scheme to Takamul: Jadwa to recover 25 of the 35 pending EOT days in exchange for partial waiver of prolongation costs. Estimated cost saving for employer: $185,000.
